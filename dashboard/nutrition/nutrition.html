Daily exercise helps to burn calories, increase metabolism, increase insulin sensistivity, and improve all around well-being. If you are in good health, try to get at least 20 minutes a day of moderate intensity exercise, or 10 minutes a day of high intensity exercise. Also consider adding step counting to your daily route. A 2016 study in the Annals of Family Medicine suggested that there may be a link between inadequate water intake and excess bodyweight. For most people, 8 cups of water (8 oz) is sufficient, and best consumed before meals to suppress appetite.
