Nutrition is the cornerstone of effective, sustainable weight loss and a healthy lifestyle. Use these charts alongside your goals to track how closely you are adhering to your diet plan.

In Depth

Daily exercise helps to burn calories, increase metabolism, increase insulin sensistivity, and improve all around well-being. If you are in good health, try to get at least 20 minutes a day of moderate intensity exercise, or 10 minutes a day of high intensity exercise. Also consider adding step counting to your daily route. Use a Fitbit, iPhone, or other fitness device to measure your steps, which will import here. Aim for about 10,000 steps a day.

A 2016 study in the Annals of Family Medicine suggested that there may be a link between inadequate water intake and excess bodyweight. For most people, 8 cups of water (8 oz) is sufficient, and best consumed before meals to suppress appetite.

Getting enough sleep will help you feel great and lose weight. Numerous studies have shown that sleep deprivation can cause food cravings, especially for high-carbohydrate foods. Aim to get 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night..
